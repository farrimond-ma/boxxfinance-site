SME Loan Approval & Rejection Statistics

UK First-Time Approval Rates

Recent data indicates that nearly 40% of first-time applications to high street banks are declined. However, the alternative lending market approves over 65% of viable applications.

Common Reasons for Business Loan Rejection

  • Poor Credit History: Defaults or CCJs against the business or directors.
  • Lack of Security: Insufficient assets to secure the loan.
  • Sector Risk: High-risk industries (e.g., hospitality) face stricter criteria.
  • Weak Cash Flow: Inability to demonstrate serviceability of the debt.

What To Do If Your Business Loan Is Rejected

A rejection from a bank is not the end of the road. It usually means you didn't fit that specific lender's rigid criteria.

  1. Diagnose the Reason: Ask the lender for specific details on the decline.
  2. Review Your Credit File: Check for errors or outdated information on your business credit report.
  3. Consider Asset-Backed Options: Leveraging assets often bypasses standard credit scoring.
  4. Explore Specialists: Niche lenders often understand sector-specific risks better than generalists.
  5. Consult a Broker: We can match your profile to the right lender.

Frequently Asked Questions About UK SME Funding

What is the average SME loan interest rate in the UK?

The average interest rate for an unsecured SME loan in the UK typically ranges between 9% and 15%, depending on credit profile, trading history and lender risk appetite. Secured loans and asset-backed facilities often carry lower rates, typically between 6% and 10%, as they provide lenders with additional security.

Rates vary significantly depending on sector, financial performance and the type of funding facility used.

Why are SME business loans rejected?

Common reasons for SME loan rejection in the UK include:

  • Declining or inconsistent revenue
  • Poor director credit history
  • High existing debt levels
  • Insufficient security for larger facilities
  • Sector risk (e.g. hospitality volatility)

Working with a specialist broker can improve approval chances by presenting applications strategically and exploring alternative funding structures.

What should I do if my business loan application is declined?

If your loan application is declined, you should:

  • Request detailed feedback from the lender
  • Review your financial statements and cash flow forecasts
  • Consider asset-backed funding options
  • Explore refinance or restructuring solutions
  • Seek guidance from a commercial finance broker

Many businesses secure funding successfully on a second application once adjustments are made.

Are asset finance rates lower than unsecured business loans?

In most cases, yes. Asset finance facilities such as hire purchase or equipment finance are typically priced lower than unsecured loans because the lender has security over the asset being financed. This reduces lender risk and can improve approval likelihood.

How often is the UK SME Funding Index updated?

The UK SME Funding Index is updated monthly to reflect changes in lender appetite, interest rate movements and sector funding trends. Commentary is revised regularly to reflect market developments.
