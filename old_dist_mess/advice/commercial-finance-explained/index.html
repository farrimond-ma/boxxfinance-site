Commercial Finance Explained: What Every Business Owner Needs to Know

Commercial finance is a broad term that covers a huge range of business funding products. It's not just about loans; it includes asset finance, invoice finance, property finance, and much more. For many business owners, the jargon can be confusing, leading them to stick with their existing bank even when better options are available elsewhere.

In this guide, we break down the key concepts to help you make informed decisions about your commercial finance options. Understanding these terms is the first step toward securing the capital you need to take your business to the next level.

Key Terms to Understand

Before applying for commercial funding, it helps to know the lingo. Here are some of the most common terms you will encounter:

LTV (Loan to Value)

This is the ratio of a loan to the value of the asset purchased. For example, if you are buying a property worth £500,000 and the lender offers an LTV of 75%, you can borrow £375,000 and will need to find the remaining £125,000 as a deposit. Higher LTVs generally mean higher risk for the lender, which can result in higher interest rates.

Term

The term is the length of time you have to repay the loan. Short-term loans might range from 3 to 18 months (often used for bridging or working capital), while long-term loans like commercial mortgages can extend up to 25 years. Choosing the right term is critical for managing your monthly cash flow.

Equity

Equity refers to the value of your ownership in the business or asset. In the context of refinancing, "releasing equity" means borrowing money against the value of an asset you already own (like a building or a piece of machinery) to generate cash for other purposes.

Personal Guarantee (PG)

Many lenders require a Personal Guarantee from the company directors, especially for unsecured loans. This means that if the business fails to repay the loan, the directors are personally liable for the debt. It is important to understand the implications of signing a PG before proceeding.

Why is Commercial Finance Important?

Access to capital is the lifeblood of any business. It allows you to:

  • Seize Opportunities: Buy bulk stock at a discount, acquire a competitor, or launch a new product line.
  • Navigate Challenges: Survive a slow season, cover an unexpected tax bill, or replace a broken piece of essential equipment.
  • Invest in Growth: Hire new staff, move to larger premises, or upgrade your technology.
