What Is a Business Loan?

A business loan is a structured borrowing facility used to fund: expansion and growth, equipment purchases, working capital, acquisitions, refinancing existing debt, or short-term cash flow requirements.

Business loans in the UK can be secured or unsecured, short-term or long-term, and structured with fixed or variable repayment profiles. The right structure depends on how your business generates revenue and manages cash flow.

Secured vs Unsecured Business Loans

Secured Business Loans: Secured against property, assets or debentures. Typically offer lower interest rates and longer terms.

Unsecured Business Loans: No asset security required, but usually shorter terms and higher cost.

We assess which option aligns with your objectives before approaching lenders.

How We Structure Business Loans

Before recommending a lender, we review your trading history, profitability, EBITDA, cash flow forecasts, and existing liabilities. We then structure the loan term, repayment profile, security package, and covenants to balance rate and sustainability.

Rate is important. Sustainability is critical.

Who Uses Business Loans?

  • Established companies refinancing existing facilities
  • Businesses consolidating debt
  • Companies funding acquisitions
  • Firms smoothing seasonal cash flow

A well-structured business loan supports growth without compromising stability.

Frequently Asked Questions

How quickly can a business loan be arranged?
Unsecured facilities may complete within 7–21 days. Secured loans depend on valuation and legal processes.

What is the typical term?
Terms range from 1 to 10 years depending on structure and security.

What interest rates apply?
Rates vary based on risk profile, security and term length.
