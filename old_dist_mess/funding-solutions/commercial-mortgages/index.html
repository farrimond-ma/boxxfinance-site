Commercial Mortgages

Purchase or refinance trading premises or investment property. Long-term commercial mortgage funding structured to preserve your capital and support your business strategy.

A commercial mortgage allows businesses and property investors to acquire or refinance commercial property while preserving capital. Ownership should strengthen long-term stability. The structure of the mortgage determines affordability, flexibility and risk exposure.

What Is a Commercial Mortgage?

A commercial mortgage is a secured loan used to purchase or refinance offices, industrial units, retail properties, mixed-use buildings, or investment property. Terms typically range from 5 to 25 years depending on occupancy and risk profile.

Owner-Occupied vs Investment Mortgages

Owner-Occupied: For businesses purchasing their own premises.

Commercial Investment: For landlords generating rental income.

Each structure requires different underwriting considerations.

How We Structure Commercial Mortgages

We assess loan-to-value ratio, rental income or trading income, interest coverage, property type, location, and exit strategy. We then structure fixed or variable rates, term lengths, repayment methods, and amortisation profiles.

A commercial mortgage should align with long-term strategy, not simply monthly affordability.

Benefits

  • Long-term stability
  • Potential capital appreciation
  • Predictable repayment profile
  • Reduced reliance on landlords
